# Letter of Intent: Why BookCheck Exists
Every great story starts with an idea, and BookCheck started with my wife's passion.

As an author, she spends countless hours pouring her heart into her books. But any writer knows that the middle stretch of a novel gets messy. Plot looses its way, character details change and these types logical cracks generally require paying an editor for a structural pass, or waiting weeks for beta readers to find the time to dig in.

I built BookCheck to give her an instant, automated "first-pass" read. Something that could map out her characters, track her timeline, and flag contradictions or duplicate passages in minutes, right from her computer.

Once we saw it in action, we realized this wasn't just useful for her. It has real value for any independent author (or editor) working on a budget, trying to polish their draft before handing it off to human eyes.

Why It's Open Source (AGPL v3)
I decided to open-source this project because I believe powerful tools shouldn't be locked behind expensive corporate paywalls. However, because this project represents genuine value and hard work, I have licensed it under the GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPL v3).

What it means is simple:

Keep it free, keep it open: Anyone is welcome to use, modify, study, and build upon this code.

No stealing: If you modify this software or integrate it into another project, your project must also be open-sourced under the same GPL v3 license. You cannot take this code, slap a new coat of paint on it, and lock it away as proprietary software.

Privacy or Speed: Your Choice
Writing is personal, and a manuscript is a massive intellectual property asset. That's why BookCheck was built with a local-first mindset. If you want absolute privacy, you can run it completely offline using Ollama. Your story never leaves your hardware. If you prefer speed and don't mind using API endpoints, you can plug in ultra-cheap cloud models for a full analysis that costs just pennies. Both paths are treated as first-class citizens.

Ultimately, BookCheck was built out of love for a writer, and my genuine hope is that it helps you with your draft, catch the sneaky mistakes, and bring your story to life.
